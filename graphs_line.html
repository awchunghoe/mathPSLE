How to Read a Line Graph

1

Read the title — What is being measured? Over what period?

2

Check the axes — Horizontal (x-axis) usually shows time. Vertical (y-axis) shows the quantity. Note the scale!

3

Locate the point — Move up from the x-value, across from the y-value. Where they meet is your data point.

4

Interpret trends — Going up (increase), going down (decrease), flat (no change).

Example: Weekly Book Sales

Questions & Solutions

Interpretation Questions
1

Q: On which day were the most books sold?

A: Saturday (80 books) — the highest point on the graph.

2

Q: What is the difference between the highest and lowest sales?

A: Highest = 80 (Sat), Lowest = 55 (Mon/Sun). Difference = 80 − 55 = 25 books.

3

Q: What is the total number of books sold in the week?

55 + 65 + 60 + 75 + 70 + 80 + 55 = 460 books

4

Q: Describe the trend from Thursday to Sunday.

A: Thursday (75) → Friday (70) decreased slightly. Friday → Saturday (80) increased. Saturday → Sunday (55) dropped sharply.

⚠️ Common Trap: Misreading the Scale

Always check: does each grid line represent 1, 2, 5, or 10 units? Never assume! In this graph, each horizontal grid line = 10 books. A point halfway between 60 and 70 = 65.

💡 Exam Tip: Use a Ruler or Straight Edge

In paper exams, use a ruler to trace up from the x-axis and across from the y-axis. For "difference" questions, mark both points first, then count the gap vertically.
