The Reverse Formulas

Forward → Reverse (working backwards):

Area = L × B → Length = Area ÷ Breadth → Breadth = Area ÷ Length
Perimeter = 2 × (L + B) → (L + B) = Perimeter ÷ 2 → then split the sum

Type 1: Given Area, Find Side

Worked Example

Question: The area of a rectangle is 72 cm². Its length is 9 cm. Find its breadth and perimeter.

1

Breadth = Area ÷ Length

= 72 ÷ 9 = 8 cm

2

Perimeter = 2 × (L + B)

= 2 × (9 + 8) = 2 × 17 = 34 cm

3

Check: Area = 9 × 8 = 72 ✓

Type 2: Given Perimeter, Find Dimensions

Worked Example

Question: The perimeter of a rectangle is 30 cm. Its length is 3 times its breadth. Find the dimensions and area.

1

L + B = Perimeter ÷ 2 = 30 ÷ 2 = 15 cm

This is the sum of length and breadth.

2

Use the ratio: Length is 3 times breadth

L : B = 3 : 1 → total parts = 4

1 unit = 15 ÷ 4 = 3.75 cm

Breadth = 3.75 cm, Length = 3 × 3.75 = 11.25 cm

3

Area = L × B

= 11.25 × 3.75 = 42.1875 cm²

Type 3: Square from Perimeter

Quick Reference
1

Side = Perimeter ÷ 4

Perimeter = 36 cm → Side = 36 ÷ 4 = 9 cm

Area = 9 × 9 = 81 cm²

⚠️ Common Trap: Forgetting to Halve the Perimeter

Perimeter = 2 × (L + B), so L + B = Perimeter ÷ 2, NOT Perimeter directly.

Common error: Perimeter = 30, L + B = 30 (wrong!) → L + B = 15 (correct!)

💡 Problem-Solving Strategy

When stuck, write the formula first. Then ask: "What do I know? What do I need to find?" Rearrange the formula to make the unknown the subject.
