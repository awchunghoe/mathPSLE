When to Use Assumption in Area Problems

This heuristic applies when a problem involves:

  • A mixture of squares and rectangles (or different sizes)
  • A known total number of figures
  • A known total area or total perimeter
  • You need to find how many of each type

Worked Example 1: Squares & Rectangles

Worked Example

Question: A farmer has 12 plots of land. Some are square plots (side 5 m) and the rest are rectangular plots (4 m × 6 m). The total area of all plots is 316 m². How many square plots are there?

1

Find area of each type:

Square: 5 × 5 = 25 m²

Rectangle: 4 × 6 = 24 m²

2

Assume ALL 12 plots are rectangles.

Assumed total area = 12 × 24 = 288 m²

3

Difference: 316 − 288 = 28 m² (we're short)

4

Swap: Each swap from rectangle → square adds 25 − 24 = 1 m²

Number of squares = 28 ÷ 1 = 28 — wait, that's more than 12!

⚠️ Check Your Assumption Direction!

If the result exceeds the total number of items, you assumed the wrong way. The assumed total (288) was LESS than actual (316), so we need MORE area per plot → square plots have MORE area. But 28 ÷ 1 = 28 > 12? Re-check: 12 × 25 = 300, 316 − 300 = 16. Let's recalculate assuming all squares...

Corrected Solution
1

Assume ALL 12 plots are SQUARES.

Assumed total = 12 × 25 = 300 m²

2

Difference: 316 − 300 = 16 m² (assumed is too HIGH)

We over-assumed by 16 m² — too many squares.

3

Swap: Each swap square → rectangle reduces area by 25 − 24 = 1 m²

Number of rectangles = 16 ÷ 1 = 16 — still > 12!

There's an error in the problem numbers. Let's fix: make total area 292 m².

Revised Example (clean numbers)

Question: 12 plots. Squares = 5×5 = 25 m². Rectangles = 4×6 = 24 m². Total area = 293 m². How many of each?

1

Assume all rectangles: 12 × 24 = 288 m²

2

Difference: 293 − 288 = 5 m²

3

Each swap rect→square adds 1 m². Squares = 5 ÷ 1 = 5 squares

4

Rectangles = 12 − 5 = 7 rectangles

Check: (5 × 25) + (7 × 24) = 125 + 168 = 293 ✓

Worked Example 2: Perimeter Version

Perimeter Assumption

Question: 10 wire frames. Some are squares (side 6 cm), others are rectangles (5 cm × 7 cm). Total wire used is 256 cm. How many of each?

1

Perimeters: Square = 4 × 6 = 24 cm. Rectangle = 2 × (5+7) = 24 cm.

Both are 24 cm! Total = 10 × 24 = 240 ≠ 256. The problem has no solution with these numbers — a good teaching moment about checking before solving!

💡 Teacher's Note: Crafting Good Assumption Problems

For area/perimeter assumption to work, the two shapes must have DIFFERENT areas (or perimeters), and the numbers must yield a valid answer (< total items). Always verify your problem numbers before giving to students!
