The Four Operations on Tables

1

Read — Identify what each row and column represents. Note the units.

2

Extract — Locate the exact cell(s) asked for. Use row + column together.

3

Compute — Perform addition, subtraction, or comparison as required.

4

Check — Does the answer make sense? Verify totals if given.

Worked Example 1: Complete the Table

Worked Example

Question: The table shows the number of books read by 4 classes. Complete it.

ClassBoysGirlsTotal
4A121527
4B18?35
4C?2240
4D141630
Total???
1

4B Girls: Total − Boys = 35 − 18 = 17

2

4C Boys: Total − Girls = 40 − 22 = 18

3

Column totals: Boys = 12+18+18+14 = 62

Girls = 15+17+22+16 = 70

Grand Total = 62 + 70 = 132 (or 27+35+40+30 = 132 ✓)

Worked Example 2: Answering Questions from Data

Worked Example

Question: Using the completed table above:

(a) Which class read the most books?
(b) How many more girls than boys read books in total?
(c) What fraction of all books were read by 4A?

1

(a) Compare totals: 4A=27, 4B=35, 4C=40, 4D=30. 4C read the most.

2

(b) More girls than boys: 70 − 62 = 8 more girls

3

(c) Fraction = 27/132 = 9/44 (÷3)

⚠️ Common Trap: Misreading Row vs. Column

Always check: am I reading across a row or down a column? Use your finger to trace from the row label AND column label to the cell.

💡 Exam Tip: Start with What You Can Fill

In "complete the table" questions, fill in the easy cells first. Each filled cell gives you more information to find the harder ones. The total row and total column are your best friends.
