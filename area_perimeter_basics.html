The Four Formulas

🔲 Rectangle

Area = Length × Breadth
A = L × B
Units: cm², m² (square units)

⬜ Square

Area = Side × Side
A = S × S = S²
Units: cm², m² (square units)

🔲 Rectangle

Perimeter = 2 × (L + B)
= L + B + L + B
Units: cm, m (linear units)

⬜ Square

Perimeter = 4 × Side
= S + S + S + S
Units: cm, m (linear units)

⚠️ Common Trap: Confusing Area and Perimeter Units

Area is always in square units (cm², m²) — it covers a surface.

Perimeter is always in linear units (cm, m) — it measures the boundary.

A common PSLE trap: giving area in cm and asking for perimeter — check units in every answer!

Worked Example 1: Rectangle

Worked Example

Question: A rectangle has length 12 cm and breadth 8 cm. Find its area and perimeter.

1

Area = L × B

= 12 × 8 = 96 cm²

2

Perimeter = 2 × (L + B)

= 2 × (12 + 8) = 2 × 20 = 40 cm

3

Check: Does the answer make sense?

Area (96) > Perimeter (40)? This comparison has no meaning — they are different units! ⚠️

Worked Example 2: Square (Finding Side from Area)

Worked Example

Question: The area of a square is 64 cm². Find the length of one side and its perimeter.

1

Area = S × S = S²

S² = 64  →  S = √64 = 8 cm

2

Perimeter = 4 × S

= 4 × 8 = 32 cm

💡 Key Insight

Finding the side from area means finding the square root. P4 pupils should memorise perfect squares up to 12²:

1²=1, 2²=4, 3²=9, 4²=16, 5²=25, 6²=36, 7²=49, 8²=64, 9²=81, 10²=100, 11²=121, 12²=144

Quick Reference: Unit Conversion

1

Length: 1 m = 100 cm

Area: 1 m² = 100 × 100 = 10 000 cm² (not 100!)

2

Converting 2 m² to cm²: 2 × 10 000 = 20 000 cm²

Converting 500 cm² to m²: 500 ÷ 10 000 = 0.05 m²
