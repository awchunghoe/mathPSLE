The Qty × Value Table

For problems with multiple items, each having a quantity and a unit value:

Total Value = Quantity × Value per unit

Set up a table with: Item | Quantity | Value each | Total Value

Worked Example 1: Two Items

Worked Example

Question: Mrs Lim bought 3.5 kg of apples at $2.80 per kg and 2.5 kg of oranges at $1.60 per kg. How much did she pay altogether?

ItemQty (kg)Value/kgTotal
Apples3.5$2.803.5 × 2.80 = $9.80
Oranges2.5$1.602.5 × 1.60 = $4.00
Total$13.80
1

Apples: 3.5 × 2.80

= 3.5 × 2.8 = 35 × 28 ÷ 100 = 980 ÷ 100 = $9.80

2

Oranges: 2.5 × 1.60

= 2.5 × 1.6 = 25 × 16 ÷ 100 = 400 ÷ 100 = $4.00

3

Total = $9.80 + $4.00 = $13.80

Worked Example 2: Finding Quantity

Reverse Calculation

Question: Water flows at 0.45 ℓ per minute into a tank. How many minutes to fill a 6.75 ℓ tank?

1

Time = Total ÷ Rate

= 6.75 ÷ 0.45 = 675 ÷ 45 = 15 minutes

2

Check: 15 × 0.45 = 15 × 45/100 = 675/100 = 6.75 ✓

Worked Example 3: Equal Total Values

Challenge

Question: A mango costs $1.20. An apple costs $0.80. Mrs Tan spent the same amount on both fruits. She bought 9 mangoes. How many apples did she buy?

1

Total spent on mangoes: 9 × $1.20 = $10.80

2

Apples: same total = $10.80, each = $0.80

Number = 10.80 ÷ 0.80 = 1080 ÷ 80 = 13.5 → 13 apples (or 13 remainder 40c)

If question expects exact: 13.5 apples, or context may say "how many whole apples"

💡 Pro Tip: The Qty×Value Table

Always draw the table. It keeps quantities and values organised. In PSLE, marks are often awarded for "correct method" even if the final arithmetic is wrong — showing the table earns method marks.
