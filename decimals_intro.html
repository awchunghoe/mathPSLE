What it is

Decimals are another way to write fractions with denominators of 10, 100, or 1000. 0.3 means 3/10. 0.07 means 7/100. P4 students must fluently convert between fractions and decimals, and understand that each decimal place is 10× smaller than the one to its left. This is the gateway to all decimal operations.

Place Value Table

3.456 = 3 ones + 4 tenths + 5 hundredths + 6 thousandths

Read it: "Three point four five six" — NOT "three point four hundred and fifty-six." Each digit is read separately after the decimal point.

Fraction ↔ Decimal Conversion

1

Fraction → Decimal: Make denominator 10, 100, or 1000

3/5 = 6/10 = 0.6   |   7/20 = 35/100 = 0.35

2

Decimal → Fraction: Read the place value of the last digit

0.3 = 3/10   |   0.25 = 25/100 = 1/4   |   0.008 = 8/1000

⚠️ Common trap

Students write 3/10 = 0.310 (adding a zero at the end). The zero changes the value! 0.310 = 310/1000, not 3/10. Train them: count the decimal places from the right — 3/10 has one zero in denominator → one decimal place → 0.3.

Comparing Decimals

Rule: Align decimal points, then compare digit by digit from left to right.

Which is bigger: 0.35 or 0.4?
Write as: 0.35 vs 0.40 (add zero to match places)
Compare: 3 tenths < 4 tenths → 0.35 < 0.4

💡 Teaching tip

Students think "35 is bigger than 4" so 0.35 must be bigger. Use money: $0.35 vs $0.40 — "Which buys more?" The zero placeholder (0.40) is critical.
