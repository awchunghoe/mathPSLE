The Key Conversions

A pie chart is a fraction visual:

Fraction = Part ÷ Total (gives fraction of the circle)
Part = Fraction × Total (finds the actual amount)
Total = Part ÷ Fraction (finds the whole)

A full circle = 360°. So angle of sector = Fraction × 360°.

Example: Favourite Fruits

Questions & Solutions

Worked Example

The pie chart shows the favourite fruits of 120 pupils.

1

Q: How many pupils chose apple?

Fraction = 1/4, Total = 120

1/4 × 120 = 30 pupils

2

Q: How many pupils chose banana?

Fraction = 1/6

1/6 × 120 = 20 pupils

3

Q: How many pupils chose grape?

Method A: 7/12 × 120 = 70 pupils

Method B: 120 − 30 − 20 = 70 pupils (check!)

4

Q: What is the angle of the apple sector?

Angle = Fraction × 360° = 1/4 × 360° = 90°

Type 2: Finding the Total from a Part

Reverse Calculation

Question: In a pie chart, 24 pupils chose red as their favourite colour. This is shown as 1/5 of the pie. How many pupils were surveyed?

1

Total = Part ÷ Fraction

Total = 24 ÷ 1/5 = 24 × 5 = 120 pupils

⚠️ Common Trap: Fractions Must Share the Same "Whole"

All sectors in a pie chart share the SAME total. You cannot add fractions from different pie charts directly. Make sure pupils know which total each pie represents.

💡 Check Strategy: Do Fractions Sum to 1?

After finding all fractions, add them: 1/4 + 1/6 + 7/12 = 3/12 + 2/12 + 7/12 = 12/12 = 1 ✓ If they don't sum to 1, there's an error.
