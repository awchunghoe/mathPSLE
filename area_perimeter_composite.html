Two Strategies for Composite Figures

✂️ Method A: Decompose (Cut)

Split the shape into 2 or more rectangles. Find each area and add them up.

Best for: L-shapes, T-shapes with clear dividing lines

📐 Method B: Complete & Subtract

Imagine a big outer rectangle. Subtract the "cut-out" or missing area.

Best for: shapes with a corner missing, door/window cutouts

Finding Missing Sides (The Key Skill)

In composite figures, not all sides are labelled. You must deduce them:

1

Opposite sides of a rectangle are equal. If one side is known, the opposite is the same.

2

Add/subtract known lengths. If a long side is 15 cm and part of it is 6 cm, the remaining part is 15 − 6 = 9 cm.

3

Mark every side before calculating — never trust a diagram at face value!

Worked Example 1: L-Shape (Decompose)

Worked Example

Question: Find the area and perimeter of this L-shape.

L-shape with overall length 18 cm, overall height 10 cm, bottom width 8 cm

1

Find missing sides:

Top-left width = 18 − 8 = 10 cm

Left-top height = 10 − 7 = 3 cm (assuming top portion is 7)

2

Method A — Decompose:

Rectangle A (top): 18 × 3 = 54 cm²

Rectangle B (bottom-left): 8 × 7 = 56 cm²

Total Area = 54 + 56 = 110 cm²

3

Perimeter: Add ALL outer sides (do not include the cut line)

18 + 3 + 10 + 7 + 8 + 10 = 56 cm

Worked Example 2: Cut-Out Corner (Complete & Subtract)

Worked Example

Question: A 12 cm × 8 cm rectangle has a 3 cm × 4 cm corner cut out. Find the remaining area and the perimeter.

1

Method B — Complete & Subtract (Area):

Big rectangle: 12 × 8 = 96 cm²

Cut-out: 3 × 4 = 12 cm²

Shaded area = 96 − 12 = 84 cm²

2

Perimeter (trick question pattern):

When you cut a corner from a rectangle, the perimeter stays the same!

Original: 2 × (12 + 8) = 40 cm

The two new edges (3 + 4) replace the two old edges (3 + 4) exactly.

⚠️ Common Trap: Including Internal Lines in Perimeter

Perimeter = only the outer boundary. Never include the dashed "cut" line.

A quick check: trace your finger around the outside of the shape only.
